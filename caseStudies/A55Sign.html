    • FCC A55 Sign installation

    Back

    We were appointed by Flintshire County Council on a scheme which consisted of replacing the existing brown Tourism Signs at Junctions 31 and 32 on the A55. The existing signs and posts were replaced with new sign faces on passive lattix posts and new supplementary white tourism signs on passive lattix posts were installed on the eastbound and westbound approaches of junction 31 and 32.

    We were issued with the drawings and site survey from the client to carry out the works. During the initial site assessment, our project manager identified that the proposed signs would not be suitable for the locations. Following a meeting with the Council and several e-mails back with the design team, the sign design issues were rectified and a second site assessment conducted in order to approve the changes.

    A traffic management plan was compiled with our key contact at Amberon who then arranged for the application of a lane closure to enable our operatives to safely carry out the sign installations.

    The work was carried out at night-time when the A55 is most quiet. We assigned a team of two operatives and a site supervisor. We appointed approved subcontractors for safety barrier removal and re-installation to enable our mini-excavator access to the sign locations to excavate for the bases. All operatives were CSCS, CPCS and SWQR card holders and received a full Health & Safety site inductions prior to work starting.

    All site drawings and utility plans were made available. The work area was marked out and scanned for potential services before excavation took place.

    The excavation for the construction of the small foundations for the signs were carried out using our 3-ton Hitachi 38u excavator operated by our trained operatives. Once the hole had been excavated the sleeves were installed and supported using a timber frame. Concrete was poured around the sleeves to secure the sign foundation. This small concreting works were carried out at all four sign locations. The concrete was allowed to cure before the sign was installed.

    On the subsequent site visit any shuttering/foundation supports were removed. Signposts and signs were erected using a Hiab lorry and MEWP operated by qualified, competent personnel.

    The area around the signs was reinstated when the installations were complete.

    The installation of the four signs took place over eight nights as programmed at the pre-start meeting. The contract value was £49,660.00 and all works were delivered on time and to the client’s specification.
