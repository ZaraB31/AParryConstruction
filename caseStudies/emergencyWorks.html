    • WCBC Emergency Works

    We have been selected to carry out a number of emergency gulley repairs for Wrexham County Borough Council along the A483. These works have often been requested at very short notice and we have never failed in being able to mobilise a team and procure the materials to carry out the repairs to the satisfaction of the client.

    As soon as the job request is received, our project manager attends site to conduct a comprehensive survey. Following this, a list of materials, plant and operatives required to carry out the repair is compiled. If materials are not held in stock at our depot, then we will source them from local approved suppliers at the best price. Our project manager will then confirm that we are able to undertake the works on the specified night to the client.

    The traffic management has usually been arranged by the client for night work when the road is at its most quiet or when cyclical traffic management is already in place for maintenance work. A lane/slip road closure is most common and once set up, our operatives receive a full Heath & Safety site induction conducted by our project manager before entering the work space.

    We would usually assign a team of two operatives to this project, along with a site supervisor – all overseen by our project manager.

    The works usually include drainage works and repairs, manhole and chamber cover replacements which have failed. The surrounding tarmac is broken away and the failed gulley pot removed. The new gulley is installed, the surround area is backfilled and the minor repairs to the road/footway surfacing is carried out using tarmac reinstatement and over banding applied to join the repair to the existing surface

    This work is carried out as part of an ongoing agreement with Wrexham County Borough Council on an ad-hoc basis. Over the course of a year the typical contract value for this work could be up to £40,000.00 depending on the repairs that need completing.

    “WCBC have used A Parry Construction on many occasions for planned and emergency works on the A483 and A5 Trunk Road network over the last 5 years. They have always been professional and efficient with the works they undertake which is typically gully repair or replacement, drainage, patching or kerbing works. They always provide a realistic timeframe for works and comply with the H+S requirements on the Trunk Road network. On the rare occasion works needs to be corrected this is undertaken promptly without any hesitation.”

    Matthew Ellison – Wrexham County Borough Council
