    • Eirias Park Lighting

    We were appointed by the principal contractor Mega Electrical NW Ltd to undertake the civils engineering works for new lighting scheme at Eirias Park. The area in which the lighting was to be upgraded had numerous risks to consider as it is a popular location with both tourists and local residents. Before works commenced an initial site survey was conducted to enable us to develop site specific risk assessments and compile a method statement to detail the way in which the project will be approached.

    The new lighting scheme consisted of below ground ducting for cabling, manhole and chamber cover replacements and installations. We assigned a team of three operatives all competent CSCS, CPCS and SWQR card holders and a site supervisor qualified to the Site Supervisor Safety Training Scheme (SSSTS).

    All excavation routes were marked out and scanned to ensure any services were highlighted. The trenches were excavated to a depth of 450mm using our own mini excavator operated by our qualified, competent operatives. The street lighting ducts were then laid along with the warning tape backfilled in a sand bed surround. Minor repairs to the road and footway surfacing were carried out using tarmac reinstatement.

    The area on the photos showing the stone monuments is particularly sensitive and the works had to take place with caution. We were asked to remove the sud from the trenches, set aside to relay when the street lighting ducts had been installed to cause minimal disturbance and damage to the area.

    All works were completed within the given timeframe and budget. As the project progressed, additional works were identified by the client and amendments made to the original scope of works. We liaised with all key stakeholders on this and developed a new schedule of work to include the requested changes. We were able to accommodate these additional works by reallocating resources to the project to ensure we could meet the new scope. Quotations for additional works were issued to the client for approval before construction commenced. An access ramp to the building with safety hand rail was one additional requirement to allow access for wheelchairs.

    Throughout the works we had weekly site meetings with Dyfed Thomas of Conwy County Borough Council along with the Project Manager and Site Supervisor for Mega Electrical NW Ltd. to provide a progress report and ensure full transparency of management information.
